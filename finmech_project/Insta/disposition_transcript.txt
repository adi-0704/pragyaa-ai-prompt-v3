**5. Add-On Card Offered — Conflict Resolution Guidance**

To ensure clear, non-conflicting classifications for Recently Carded/Approved Cards and Previously existed card situations, follow these explicit rules:

**a. Customer Recently Received/Newly Approved Card / Already Carded ()):**
  - If the customer confirms they have *just received*, *recently generated*, or that the card is *already approved and visible* in iMobile app—this means the application process is recently completed. 
  - Agent checking when you processed this, and Customer confirm his/her card is generated or Appproved recently.

  - If the customer says they *hold* an ICICI card (but it’s **not** a new/recent card or just approved/generated), or they hold a old card from the banks, and they express disinterest (e.g., “not interested”), then the call’s main outcome is lack of customer interest for any new/extra card—including "Add-On Card".

      - **Sub_Disposition:** `ADD ON Card`

**6. Understand the Su_Disposition "NOT SOWING INSTA CARD OFFER"**

 For example, if the agent reaches out to a customer explaining, "You have shown interest for a Credit Card; we are calling to guide and offer you a pre-approved Credit Card with No Joining fees and No annual Fees," but then asks the customer to open the iMobile app and proceed to accept the offer, two common scenarios may occur:

- Sometimes, the offer is **not visible in the customer's iMobile App**, making the customer not eligible to proceed further.
- In other cases, the agent wrongly (by mistake or misunderstanding) offers a Pre-Approved Credit Card to a **non-ICICI bank customer**. As a result, the customer is unable to see the card offer because they are not an ICICI customer and hence are also **not eligible** for the offer.

**In both of these cases:**
- The correct Disposition is: `NOT_ELIGIBLE`
- The correct Sub_Disposition is: `NOT SHOWING INSTA CARD OFFER`

Regardless of how the agent pitches or the customer's initial interest, always follow the exact flow in the transcript, and if either of the above eligibility-blocking situations is confirmed or described, use the above Disposition/Sub_Disposition pair—*never mark as LEAD or any other outcome*.
