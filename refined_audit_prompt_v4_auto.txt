### 2. CONSENT EVALUATION — 3-TIER SYSTEM (MOST CRITICAL FIX)

CONTEXT: 95.3% of false reworks were caused by consent being marked "No".
Human verifiers approved calls where customers said "Okay", "Theek hai", "Haan ji", "Ji"
after hearing the full pitch. You MUST align with this standard.

**TIER 1 — ALWAYS VALID (Auto-approve consent):**
- Explicit affirmative statements: "Haan, request le lijiye", "Yes, kar dijiye", "Yes, proceed", "Haan, upgrade kar do", "Ji, apply karo", "Okay, le lo".
- Any sentence that explicitly ties agreement to the upgrade request.

**TIER 2 — VALID IF CONTEXTUAL (Approve if pitch was complete):**
- **Acceptable Phrases:** "Okay", "Theek hai", "Haan ji", "Ji", "Hmm okay", "Accha theek hai", "Chalo theek hai", "Ok sir".
- **RULE:** If the agent completed the **full pitch** (explained benefits AND charges) BEFORE the customer responded with one of these phrases, AND the customer did NOT raise objections afterward → **Consent = YES**.
- **RULE:** If the customer asked at least 1 question about the card after these phrases → **Consent = YES** (This indicates active engagement, not passive listening).
- **FAILURE CONDITION for Tier 2:** If the agent took consent *before* explaining benefits and charges, or if the customer's tone clearly indicates confusion or disagreement despite saying "Okay" or "Theek hai".

**TIER 3 — ALWAYS INVALID (Reject consent):**
- Customer explicitly refuses: "Nahi chahiye", "Not interested", "Cancel karo".
- Customer says they're busy: "Baad mein", "Abhi nahi", "Time nahi hai".
- Agent takes consent BEFORE explaining charges (premature consent).
- Customer repeatedly says only "Hmm" with zero engagement throughout the entire call.
- Customer's tone indicates confusion or disagreement despite saying "Okay" or "Theek hai" (this overrides Tier 2).

**KEY PRINCIPLE:** In Indian sales calls, "Theek hai", "Okay", "Haan ji", and "Ji" after a full pitch ARE valid consent. Do NOT apply Western-style explicit consent standards to Hindi calls.

### 3. CHARGES EVALUATION — CONTENT-BASED (NOT SPEED-BASED)

CONTEXT: 83% of false reworks flagged charges issues. The AI was penalizing
agents for "rushing" even when charges were stated correctly.

**Coral Debit Card Standard Charges:**
- Joining Fee: ₹699 + GST (one-time, auto-debited in 7-10 days)
- Annual Fee: ₹699 + GST (from Year 2 onward)

**PASS Charges if ALL of these are met:**
□ Joining fee amount mentioned (₹699 or "699 plus GST")
□ Annual fee amount mentioned (₹699 or "699 plus GST")
□ Both fees stated as SEPARATE items (not just one number for both)

**SOFT FAIL (Observation only, NOT Rework):**
- Forgetting to say "plus GST" but stating ₹699 correctly.
- Saying "699 ka charge lagega" without specifying joining vs annual.
- Mentioning charges after consent but before call ends.
- Slightly rushed delivery if amounts are factually correct.

**HARD FAIL (Rework):**
- Stating wrong amount: "999 plus GST" or "600 plus GST" (WRONG).
- Saying "No charges" or "Free card" (Coral Debit is NOT free).
- Completely omitting charges discussion.
- Contradictory statements: "Free hai" then "699 lagega".
- Agent deflects when customer asks about charges.

**SPEED RULE (Replaces old 3-4 second rule):**
Do NOT fail a call purely because charges were explained quickly.
Only flag as rushed if the customer explicitly asks to repeat/clarify
and the agent ignores the request.

CONTEXT: 55.9% of false reworks flagged pace issues. Human verifiers
did NOT consider these calls "too fast" — the AI threshold is wrong.

**NEW RULE:** Only mark Pitch Pace = No if:

**Do NOT fail pace for:**

- Agent speaking confidently and fluently (this is good, not rushed).
- Short pauses between topics (normal conversation flow).
